        ## AI for Climate Governance, Earth Observation and Sustainability Intelligence

        I am a postdoctoral researcher at Tsinghua University's Institute of Energy, Environment and Economy, supported by the Shuimu Tsinghua Scholarship. My research develops artificial intelligence systems to support climate governance, greenhouse gas mitigation and sustainability decision-making.

        I work at the intersection of machine learning, remote sensing, mathematical modeling and public policy, with applications ranging from methane emissions monitoring and industrial infrastructure analysis to climate scenario assessment and sustainability intelligence.

        Prior to joining Tsinghua University, I completed a PhD in Mathematics and Computer Science at École Polytechnique (CMAP) and ISEP under the supervision of Éric Moulines, Jérémie Sublime and Thomas Lauvaux.

        My doctoral research led to the development of a Dynamic and Intelligent Methane Emissions Inventory, an AI-driven framework designed to automatically characterize methane-emitting behaviors across the oil and gas industry using satellite observations and machine learning.

        I have contributed to initiatives in collaboration with the United Nations Environment Programme (UNEP), including work connected to the International Methane Emissions Observatory (IMEO), the Oil and Gas Methane Partnership (OGMP), and the SDG-Meter project, recognized among the IRCAI-UNESCO Top 100 AI solutions for sustainable development.

        I am also a core team member of Climate Change AI (CCAI), a global non-profit catalyzing impactful work at the intersection of climate change and machine learning.
